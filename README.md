# Frankenstein Reimagined – Interactive Visual Narrative Website

## Overview
This project is a Humanities and Social Studies (HASS) digital humanities website that reinterprets Mary Shelley’s *Frankenstein (1818)* through an interactive, scroll-based visual narrative. Instead of presenting the story in a traditional linear text format, this website uses imagery, layered storytelling, and user-driven scrolling to reshape how users engage with themes of empathy, moral responsibility, and societal rejection.

The goal is to challenge the common misconception of the “monster” archetype and encourage deeper reflection on how narrative format influences interpretation.

---

## Objectives
- Reframe *Frankenstein* beyond its horror stereotype
- Explore moral responsibility between creator and creation
- Investigate how digital storytelling affects reader empathy
- Demonstrate how medium shapes interpretation of literature

---

## Features

### 1. Interactive Scroll Narrative
A vertically scrolling experience that reveals the story progressively, encouraging reflection at each stage rather than passive reading.

### 2. Visual Storytelling
Key scenes are represented through curated images and layout design to enhance emotional interpretation.

### 3. Dual Perspective Framing
The narrative shifts between Victor Frankenstein and the Creature to highlight contrasting moral viewpoints.

### 4. Minimal Text Design
Short, intentional text fragments are used to guide interpretation rather than explicitly explain the story.

### 5. Immersive User Experience
The layout encourages users to interpret meaning themselves, rather than being told what to think.

## Project Insight
This project demonstrates that interpretation is not fixed within the text itself, but is shaped by how the story is delivered. By transforming *Frankenstein* into an interactive visual experience, users are encouraged to reconsider assumptions about the Creature and reflect on the ethical responsibilities of creation and rejection.
